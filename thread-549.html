Things have changed on the BBC side with the advent of their Video Factory initiative.  HLS HD streams will return in get_iplayer 2.95.  It is already implemented in the development version. There is no HD RTMP stream in this case - the web site is using HDS.

Q: When is HD not HD?  A: When the BBC says "Watch in HD".  On the web site that now gets you 960x540 2800kbps 50fps video. The BBC are calling this HD and asserting that it's equivalent to the older 720p streams because of the higher frame rate. get_iplayer will have to adopt the BBC newspeak to track with the iPlayer site, but it's going to be confusing because: a) the notion of any video <720p being called HD will seem a bit alien; b) the older 720p 25fps streams are still around for the moment. OTOH, In terms of bandwidth the old and new HD streams are roughly equivalent, which is the more important metric for get_iplayer. Plus, the new 720p streams are also 50fps, which makes for ridiculously big downloads - get ready for "vhd" mode.

In 2.94 the RTMP stream data is used to find the HLS HD streams.  No RTMP HD, no HLS HD. That has changed with the advent of Video Factory streams.  As for why there is no RTMP HD stream, who knows?  They haven't used it on the web site for some time, so maybe they just didn't bother producing RTMP HD for this programme. The CBeebies crowd aren't going to be worried about the extra pixels. Stream data for kiddie progs can be squirrelly sometimes as well, for reasons unknown.
