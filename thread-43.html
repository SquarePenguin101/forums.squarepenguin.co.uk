Sorry, but you need to back up and start over. You haven't even said what goes wrong with perl.exe or what you were doing when the error appeared. The procmon log you sent is, unfortunately, useless because your filter restricted capture only to events from the get_iplayer installer. Even if you were trying to monitor perl.exe when launched by the installer, your filter prevented any trace of it from appearing in the log. Also, if you don't need a separate installation of Strawberry Perl - and I'm guessing you don't - then uninstall it and remove all traces of it from your system. And don't swap around different versions of perl.exe. There is nothing wrong with the version provided, and we can't support you unless you install only that version.

I'm going to take a guess that what you were trying to say was perl.exe wouldn't execute from inside the get_iplayer installer. A likely culprit is your anti-virus (Avast), which can interfere with installation, so completely disable it before trying to install again. However, your procmon log also shows accesses to www.007guard.com, which is sign that your machine has been infected by malware. Google it for possible solutions.
