In general, it's not a good idea to configure your output directory on a network drive.  Remember that every file is pushed back and forth across the wire 5 times by default. When your drive or system can't keep up, you will encounter problems like this with ffmpeg during re-muxing and with AtomicParsley during tagging. Either download to internal HD and move the finished files (can be automated with --command), or use --raw to skip re-muxing and tagging. The latter may still have glitches since a slow drive can cause problems for rtmpdump as well.
