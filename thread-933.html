As the documentation tells you, --aactomp3 is only applied to radio programmes. You will need to use one of the many available media converter utilities to rip the audio from a TV programme after download. If you use a command-line converter like ffmpeg, you could automatically invoke it with --command, but that hardly seems worth the bother for a one-off.

If you download the video, then you can use ffmpeg or even mp4box to losslessly extract the aac audio. The best audio is contained within the 50fps video and is usually 125kbps aac. The 25fps video is usually 93kbps.

To download the 50vps videos you'll need the 2.95dev version and select the hvfhd1 mode:
--modes=hvfhd1
