Perhaps you have a crippled version of ffmpeg ("Not yet implemented in FFmpeg" should be a red flag), but there is no way for us to know. Moreover: a) you are not using 2.96 - look at your own output; b) the PID in your example is no longer available, so cannot be tested. Upgrade to 2.96, pick an available programme as an example, and above all follow our instructions. Otherwise we have no idea what is actually happening on your system.
